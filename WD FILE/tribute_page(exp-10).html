Why plants matter

Plants make air breathable, provide food and shelter, and shape landscapes. They are the first step in every food chain and the backbone of healthy ecosystems.

Beyond utility, plants improve mental wellbeing: a single indoor plant can reduce stress and brighten a room. Respecting plants is respecting our future.

Quick ways to care for plants

Small consistent actions give the best results. Start with a resilient plant and learn its needs: light, water, soil and occasional feeding.

  • Place in bright, indirect light for most houseplants.
  • Allow soil to dry slightly between watering to avoid root rot.
  • Use well-draining soil and a pot with drainage holes.
  • Prune dead leaves and rotate plants for even growth.

Plants to begin with

Succulents, spider plants and pothos are forgiving and great for beginners. They tolerate irregular care while teaching you the basics.
